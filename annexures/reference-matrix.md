# Annexure B: IDRBT Published Tenders vs. IKCON Award — Negative Evidence Analysis

**Method**: IDRBT's tender page (https://www.idrbt.ac.in/tenders/) lists all current and archived published tenders. We checked all entries on this page for any mention of `.bank.in`, domain registry, or IKCON.

**Result**: No tender was published for the `.bank.in` domain registry portal or related services. This is consistent with the CashlessConsumer report's finding that the contract was awarded without competitive tender.

**IDRBT Tenders found on page (as of 2026-07-11)**:
- Concurrent Auditor appointment
- Modular Furniture supply
- HSM procurement (multiple rounds)
- Facility Services
- Various academic/IT procurement

**Negative evidence confirmation**: The absence of a `.bank.in` portal tender where all other IDRBT procurement is published constitutes strong negative evidence that the contract was awarded without competitive process. If it were competitively tendered, the tender notice should have appeared on this page alongside all others.

# Annexure D: Key Legal Framework Analysis

## IDRBT's Legal Status

| Legal Instrument | Relevance |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Societies Registration Act, 1860 | IDRBT registered under this Act — designed for literary, scientific, and charitable societies |
| RBI Act, 1934 | RBI's parent act — does not specifically authorize subsidiary creation |
| RTI Act, 2005 | Section 2(h): "public authority" test — IDRBT likely fails this test as a society |
| Companies Act, 2013 | Not applicable to IDRBT; ReBIT and NIPL registered under this |
| Section 8 Companies Act, 2013 | Legal form for IFTAS, RBIH, NPCI (not-for-profit) |
| General Financial Rules (GFR) | Not applicable to societies; only govt departments |
| CAG's audit mandate | National Financial Reporting Authority (NFRA) doesn't cover private/society entities |
